The Bangladesh Liberation War (1971)

The Bangladesh Liberation War (March 26 – December 16, 1971) was a nine-month conflict that led to East Pakistan breaking away to become the independent nation of Bangladesh.

Core Timeline:

  • The Cause: The West Pakistani ruling elite refused to hand over power after the East Pakistani Awami League, led by Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, won the 1970 democratic election.
  • The Crackdown: On March 25, 1971, the Pakistani military launched Operation Searchlight, initiating a brutal genocide against Bengali civilians, intellectuals, and minorities.
  • The Resistance: Bengali defectors and citizens formed a guerrilla army called the Mukti Bahini to fight back.
  • The Victory: India officially joined the war in December, leading to the total surrender of Pakistani forces on December 16, 1971 (Victory Day).
